## Overview
GoPick Manual is a documentation-first product UI that borrows the clarity of a modern SaaS dashboard without becoming visually busy. The visual identity is built on bright white surfaces, cool slate neutrals, and one assertive red accent that signals brand, primary actions, and active states.

The experience splits into two related moods. The landing page is slightly more promotional, using soft red and blue-tinted gradients, hover lift, and icon color accents to make the manual feel welcoming. The inner manual pages are calmer and more utilitarian: fixed headers, pale slate page backgrounds, white reading cards, and a strong emphasis on scannability.

This interface should feel orderly, contemporary, and dependable. It is not playful, luxurious, or highly expressive. The design earns trust by staying legible, spacious, and consistent.

## Colors
The palette is led by a single brand red and a broad slate neutral system.

- **Primary (`#e50914`)** is reserved for the most important action, active navigation, small emphasis labels, and branded highlights.
- **Primary Hover (`#b3060f`)** deepens the red for hover and pressed feedback without changing the visual language.
- **Neutrals** do most of the work: white and near-white surfaces carry the layout, while slate text establishes hierarchy from headline to metadata.
- **Dark Surfaces** appear only in focused areas such as the access section and the footer. These are deep slate rather than pure black.
- **Support Accents** such as indigo, emerald, cyan, amber, violet, teal, and blue appear inside feature icons, role badges, and status summaries. They are secondary accents, not alternate brand colors.

Use red sparingly. A screen should usually have one obvious red focal point, not many. The overall impression should remain neutral and documentation-oriented.

## Typography
The system uses **Inter** and a standard sans-serif fallback stack throughout. Typography is direct and practical rather than expressive.

- **Display and headline text** use heavy weights to create immediate hierarchy in heroes, section headers, and major page titles.
- **Body copy** stays in the 14px to 18px range with generous line height for long-form reading.
- **Labels and metadata** are often semi-bold or bold. The smallest labels use uppercase tracking for navigation aids, badges, and eyebrow text.
- **Monospace text** is used selectively for hierarchy diagrams and other structured reference content.

Type should feel crisp, contemporary, and easy to scan. Avoid decorative type treatments, condensed faces, or excessive weight variety.

## Layout
The layout follows a centered, fixed-max-width documentation model.

- Outer content sits inside a wide centered container with 16px mobile padding and 24px desktop padding.
- Major landing-page sections use generous vertical spacing, typically 64px on mobile and 96px on desktop.
- Inner manual pages use a five-column desktop composition with a one-column sticky sidebar and a four-column content area.
- Cards, grids, and repeated content blocks rely on 16px to 24px gaps, keeping the rhythm tight enough for documentation but never cramped.
- Headers are fixed at 64px tall, and sticky sidebar content begins below a generous top offset so it never collides with the header.

On mobile, navigation and sidebars convert to overlay drawers rather than collapsing the content into dense stacked controls.

## Elevation & Depth
Depth is soft and functional. The UI does not use dramatic shadows or heavy skeuomorphism.

- Most hierarchy is created with white-on-slate tonal separation and thin borders.
- Standard cards use a very light shadow and pale border so they read as floating surfaces without feeling detached from the page.
- Hoverable cards add a stronger but still soft slate shadow plus a small upward translation.
- Overlays such as search panels and mobile drawers use deeper shadows and a blurred dark backdrop to establish focus.
- The dark access cards use translucent white surfaces over a deep slate gradient to add atmosphere without leaving the overall design language.

If a surface does not need emphasis, favor border-and-tone hierarchy over stronger shadow.

## Shapes
The shape language is soft, rectangular, and restrained.

- Most buttons, cards, drawers, and utility controls use 8px to 12px radii.
- Large content wells and special containers may stretch to 16px or 24px radii.
- Pills and role badges use full rounding.
- Corners should never become ultra-sharp or overly bubbly. The system wants gentle softness, not playful roundness.

This is a polished business interface, so shapes should support calm readability and familiar interaction.

## Components
### Buttons
Primary buttons are solid red with white text and a soft branded shadow. Secondary buttons are white with slate text and a subtle border, picking up red only on hover. Icon buttons follow the same white-card utility treatment.

### Cards
The system relies heavily on cards. Default cards are white with pale borders and light shadows. Informational sub-cards may switch to muted slate backgrounds. Hoverable cards lift slightly and increase shadow rather than changing color dramatically.

### Navigation
Top navigation is light and minimal. Default links are muted slate; hover and active states turn red. Mobile navigation appears in a right-side drawer over a dark blurred backdrop. Inner-page navigation uses a sticky white sidebar panel on desktop and a floating launcher plus slide-in panel on mobile.

### Search
The global search pattern is a compact white trigger in the header that opens a centered white panel. Results are rendered as muted slate cards with red hover emphasis, keeping the interaction aligned with the rest of the documentation system.

### Status and Role Markers
Role badges and icon panels use tinted color backgrounds with stronger matching foregrounds. These accents help categorization, but the surrounding card and page structure remains neutral so the screen never feels multicolored.

### Tables and Structured Content
Large data tables use muted slate headers, thin dividers, sticky structural columns, and minimal row striping. Structured content blocks such as hierarchy diagrams, rules, and warnings use subdued tinted surfaces instead of high-contrast alert styling.

## Do's and Don'ts
- Do keep the UI predominantly white and slate, with red as the clear action signal.
- Do use spacing generously so documentation remains readable and calm.
- Do rely on borders, tonal separation, and subtle shadows before introducing stronger visual effects.
- Do keep component corners consistently soft and rectangular.
- Don't turn support accent colors into competing call-to-action colors.
- Don't fill a screen with multiple red elements of equal importance.
- Don't use heavy gradients outside hero or dark emphasis sections.
- Don't make hover states feel jumpy; motion should stay quick, smooth, and understated.
